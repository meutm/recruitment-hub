Choose your track

Do not apply to everything. Apply to the role you can own.

The hub separates candidates into two selection funnels. Participants enter the simulation as delegates or journalists. Content candidates shape the institutional logic, procedures and intellectual quality of the event.

01

Participants

For candidates who want to experience the simulation from inside the political room: European Parliament, EU Council or journalism.

  • Primary and secondary role preference
  • 150-word role essays
  • Previous MEU/MUN experience
  • Motivation video and candidate dossier
02

Content

For candidates who can carry the simulation's architecture: presidency, legal direction, commission roleplay and procedural leadership.

  • Content role preference
  • Leadership and coordination evidence
  • Debating, mediation and chairing experience
  • Simulation contribution and academic judgment

Selection psychology

This is not an attendance list. It is a room with standards.

The best applications will make the reviewers feel three things quickly: this person understands the role, this person can be trusted under pressure, and the event would be weaker without them.

01

Role clarity

We look for candidates who know what seat they are trying to earn.

02

Presence

The short video shows judgment, confidence and sincerity faster than text alone.

03

Proof

Experience matters, but so does evidence of growth, discipline and preparation.

04

Contribution

The strongest candidates explain what they will add to the room, not only what they will take from it.

Data governance summary

Data is stored in a Google Sheet and candidate files are stored in a restricted Google Drive folder. Access should be limited to the recruitment team. Recommended retention: 12 months after the selection process, unless the candidate is selected and data is needed for event operations.
